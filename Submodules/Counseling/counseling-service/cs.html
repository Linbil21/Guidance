Guidance is a Place to Belong.

It's a common misconception that only troublesome students belong in guidance, but this is far from the truth.

The purpose of guidance is to help students understand themselves better and make changes and plans for the future that they are unable to imagine, not to punish them. Career guidance, for example, is most suited for individuals who are driven to achieve their objectives. However, you may always ask for support when facing financial, familial, or other issues without fear of being judged.

Guidance Is For Seekers.

Guidance is not for tutorials; it is a place to learn. It's not always a bad thing to ask for help, but even if you can, most of the time they will just walk you through the steps.

Many students want to know the routes rather than the whole blueprint for honing their hunting abilities and reaching their goals; nonetheless, the hard work and lessons are your responsibility, not theirs.
